Computes the constant loglikelihood from the sample and ask Biogeme to include it in the output file. 

It assumes that the full choice set is available for each observation. 
\begin{DoxyParams}{Parameters}
{\em availability} & A \href{http://docs.python.org/py3k/tutorial/datastructures.html#dictionaries}{\tt dictionary} mapping each alternative I\+D with its availability condition. \\
\hline
{\em choice\+Set} & list containing the alternatives in the choice set. \\
\hline
{\em choice} & expression producing the id of the chosen alternative. \\
\hline
{\em iterator} & An iterator on the data file. \\
\hline
\end{DoxyParams}
\begin{DoxyReturn}{Returns}
loglikelihood of a logit model where the only parameters are the alternative specific constant. If $n_i$ is the number of time alternative $i$ is chosen, then it is given by \[ \mathcal{L} = \sum_i n_i \ln(n_i) - n \ln(n) \] where $ n = \sum_i n_i $ is the total number of observation. 
\end{DoxyReturn}
